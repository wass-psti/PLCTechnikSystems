Cement Plant Rotopacker
Automation & SCADA System

Sector: Heavy Industry | In Progress
Project Overview

Watchdog Automation is currently executing the automation and SCADA system development for a cement plant Rotopacker packaging system — a critical component in the final stage of cement production.

The project focuses on the complete automation of conveying systems and material handling equipment feeding the Rotopacker unit, ensuring continuous, synchronized, and high-efficiency cement packaging operations. Cement packaging plants operate under high-throughput and continuous production conditions, where any interruption in material flow directly impacts plant output. This project addresses these challenges by delivering a fully integrated automation system designed for reliability, performance, and scalability.

Project Scope
Material Handling Automation Conveyor & Elevator Control Rotopacker Interface & Synchronization PLC Engineering (S7-1500) SCADA Development (WinCC Professional) Process Interlocking & Sequencing Motor Control & Protection Alarm Management Historical Trending & Data Logging
Full Automation Scope

The system covers the entire material flow from bulk cement transfer up to the Rotopacker interface. Each piece of equipment is automated and interlocked to ensure smooth, coordinated operation — eliminating bottlenecks and optimizing material flow into the packaging unit.

Equipment & Systems Covered
Belt conveyors and transfer systems
Bucket elevators and vertical transport systems
Surge bins and feeding mechanisms
Rotary feeders and discharge control
Material flow interlocking and sequencing
Motor control and protection systems
Equipment status monitoring and diagnostics
Rotopacker system interface and synchronization
Automation & Control System Architecture

The system is built on a high-performance Siemens SIMATIC S7-1500 PLC platform, designed to support large-scale industrial operations. The S7-1500 was selected for its high-speed processing, advanced diagnostics, and suitability for complex, multi-subsystem automation in demanding industrial environments.

Supervisory control is implemented through Siemens WinCC Professional SCADA, managing approximately 5,000 process and power tags — reflecting the complexity and scale of the cement packaging system.

Control Philosophy & System Design

The automation system is engineered using a hierarchical and modular control philosophy, ensuring flexibility, maintainability, and ease of future expansion. This architecture enables operators to manage the entire packaging line through a single, unified SCADA interface.

Key Design Principles
Distributed control of material handling subsystems
Centralized SCADA supervision and control
Intelligent interlocking to prevent equipment conflicts
Fail-safe operation and alarm prioritization
Real-time monitoring of equipment status and material flow
