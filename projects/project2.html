Bulk Water Distribution
Monitoring & Telemetry System

Location: Cebu, Philippines | Completed
Project Overview

PLC Systems Technik Inc. successfully engineered and deployed a remote water monitoring and telemetry system for a major bulk water supplier, enabling real-time monitoring of water distribution quality and flow conditions across multiple injection points within the supply network.

The project involved the supply, installation, and integration of flow measurement instrumentation, water quality analyzers, telemetry communication systems, PLC automation, and a custom-developed remote monitoring platform. The primary objective was to provide the utility operator with centralized visibility of water quality parameters and distribution flow performance across geographically distributed injection points — with the farthest injection point located approximately 25 kilometers from the central monitoring station, requiring a robust long-distance telemetry infrastructure.

Project Scope
Flow Instrumentation Supply & Installation Water Quality Analyzer Integration PLC Automation at Each Injection Point Long-Distance Telemetry Infrastructure Pressure Monitoring Custom SCADA Platform Development Remote Monitoring Software (Watchdog DataSpy) Commissioning & Handover
Distributed Water Injection Network

The water distribution system consists of four separate water injection points, each located in different areas of the supply network. Each injection point was independently equipped with automation controllers, field instrumentation, and telemetry modules — forming a distributed monitoring architecture capable of transmitting process data from all remote locations to a single central monitoring facility.

The geographic spread of the injection points — with one located 25 kilometers away — required a reliable communication infrastructure designed for uninterrupted long-distance data transmission regardless of network conditions in the field.

Instrumentation & Measurement Systems
Flow Measurement

Siemens MAG 8000 electromagnetic flowmeters were installed at each of the four injection points to provide accurate, continuous measurement of water flow rate and totalized volume within the distribution network. The MAG 8000 was selected for its proven reliability in water utility applications and its ability to provide accurate volume measurement even under varying flow conditions.

Water Quality Monitoring

Endress+Hauser CDP20 analyzers were deployed at each injection point to continuously measure key water quality parameters, ensuring compliance with water treatment standards and enabling real-time monitoring of distribution quality conditions across the network.

Pressure Monitoring

Pressure transmitters were installed within the distribution pipeline network at each injection point to monitor system hydraulic pressure and enable early detection of pressure anomalies, leakage events, or supply disruptions within the distribution network.

Automation & Telemetry Architecture

Each injection point was equipped with a local automation controller based on the Siemens SIMATIC S7-1200 PLC, responsible for collecting data from all field instrumentation and managing local process signals at each distributed site.

To enable reliable long-distance communication between the remote injection points and the central monitoring facility, the system utilizes EWON Flexy 205 industrial telemetry modules. These modules provide secure remote connectivity for process data transmission, allowing real-time field data to flow continuously from each remote location to the central monitoring station — giving operators full visibility of the entire water distribution network at all times.

Watchdog DataSpy Remote Monitoring Platform

A key deliverable of the project was the development of a custom remote monitoring platform called Watchdog DataSpy, designed specifically for monitoring distributed water infrastructure. The platform was developed using a VB.NET software architecture and integrated with Siemens WinCC Unified SCADA to provide a unified monitoring environment accessible from the central facility.

Watchdog DataSpy enables operators to view all four injection point data streams from a single central interface — allowing rapid detection of abnormal conditions, quality deviations, or flow irregularities anywhere in the distribution network without requiring personnel to physically visit remote sites.
